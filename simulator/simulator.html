What If Simulations

What if temperature changes?

To test temperature on its own, humidity was kept constant at 60%.

At 15°C, the predicted wildfire risk was 15%.
At 25°C, the risk increased to 43%.

This shows a clear positive relationship between temperature and wildfire probability. As temperature rises, conditions become drier, increasing fire risk.

What if humidity changes?

To test humidity independently, temperature was kept constant at 20°C.

At 80% humidity, wildfire risk was 21%.
At 40% humidity, risk increased to 33%.

This shows a negative relationship between humidity and wildfire risk. Lower humidity dries vegetation, making ignition more likely.

What if both change?

When temperature increased to 25°C and humidity decreased to 40%, the predicted wildfire risk rose to 50%, the highest result recorded.

This suggests that warm and dry conditions combine to significantly increase wildfire probability.
